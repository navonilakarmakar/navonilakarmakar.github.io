MS Thesis

Hydrodynamic Cloud–Wind Simulations

Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics (MPA), Garching, Germany

Supervisors: Prof. Dr. Volker Springel & Dr. Alankar Dutta
Computational Astrophysics & Galaxy Formation and Evolution Research Groups

MS thesis project focused on hydrodynamic simulations of gas clouds under realistic galactic wind conditions, incorporating multi-cloud interactions. The goal is to develop robust theoretical predictions for cloud survival timescales, cloud morphological evolution, and the role of cloud–cloud interactions in shaping the circumgalactic medium. Work involves running and analysing simulations with OpenGadget3 and PLUTO, post-processing with ParaView, and comparing results against analytic cloud-crushing models.

Remote Intern

Infrared Photometry of Stellar Cluster Barba 2

European Southern Observatory (ESO), Garching, Germany

Supervisor: Dr. Morten Andersen

Characterising the intermediate and low-mass stellar content of Barba 2, a newly discovered supergiant-rich Galactic cluster. Imaging data in H and Ks bands from Gemini FLAMINGOS-2 were reduced using the Dragons-Flamingos2 pipeline and Astropy. Performed aperture photometry on cluster members using photutils, calibrated against 2MASS photometric standards. Ongoing work includes determining individual stellar masses and cluster age from the pre-main-sequence turn-on mass, quantifying mass segregation via variations in the derived Mass Function, and constructing a complete census of cluster membership.

LIGO GET-GO Fellow

Glitch Mitigation in Precessing GW Searches

Institute of Gravitation and Cosmology, University of Portsmouth, UK

Supervisors: Prof. Laura Nuttall & Dr. Rahul Dhurkunde

Fellowship project on mitigating the effect of instrumental glitches in searches for gravitational waves from precessing compact binary systems. Gained hands-on experience with matched filtering, signal consistency tests, coincident detection, and ranking statistics using PyCBC. Studied the mathematical formalism of harmonic decomposition of precessing waveforms and the construction of precessing template banks. Conducted precessing searches on several days of LIGO O3 data on the CIT cluster, identified GPS times of glitches impacting precessing searches, characterised glitch types, and estimated detection sensitivity for a simulated population of injections at a fixed FAR. Compared the sensitivity improvement of the new model against existing general searches.

PyCBC Matched Filtering Gravitational Waves Precessing Waveforms LIGO O3 Python

Semester Project

Structural & Morphological Analysis of Galaxies

IISER Kolkata — Astronomy Lab Course

Course Project

Comprehensive morphological study of three galaxies representing different Hubble types: NGC 2967 (Sc spiral), NGC 3351 (SBb barred spiral), and NGC 3379 (E1 elliptical). Star Formation in Young Cluster NGC 2316

Aryabhatta Research Institute of Observational Sciences (ARIES), Nainital, India

Supervisor: Dr. Saurabh

Observational study of star formation in the young open cluster NGC 2316, using the 3.6 m Devasthal Optical Telescope with imaging and spectroscopy via TANSPEC. Performed UVBRI PSF photometry of cluster stars using DAOPHOT II, calibrated with Landolt standard SA 98 to determine true stellar magnitudes. Conducted a 2MASS near-infrared photometric study for cluster identification, kinematic membership analysis from GAIA proper motion data, and astrometry using IRAF, GAIA Tool, and WCSTools. Performed isochrone fitting with the intrinsic Zero-Age Main Sequence on the HR diagram for morphological age determination.
