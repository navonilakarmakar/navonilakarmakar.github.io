MS Thesis

Hydrodynamic Cloud–Wind Simulations

Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics, Garching, Germany

Supervisors: Prof. Dr. Volker Springel & Dr. Alankar Dutta
Research Groups: Computational Astrophysics & Galaxy Formation and Evolution

MS thesis project focused on hydrodynamic simulations of gas clouds under realistic galactic wind conditions, incorporating multi-cloud interactions. The goal is to develop robust theoretical predictions for cloud survival timescales, cloud morphological evolution, and the role of cloud–cloud interactions in shaping the circumgalactic medium. Work involves running and analysing simulations with PLUTO and Arepo, post-processing with ParaView, and comparing results against analytic cloud-crushing models.

OpenGadget3 PLUTO ParaView Python Hydrodynamics Galaxy Formation CGM

Remote Intern

Stellar Content of Newly Discovered Cluster Barba 2

European Southern Observatory (ESO), Garching, Germany

Supervisor: Dr. Morten Andersen

Project focused on characterising the intermediate and low-mass stellar content of Barba 2, a supergiant- rich Galactic cluster. Imaging data in H and Ks bands from Gemini FLAMINGOS-2 were reduced using the Dragons-Flamingos2 pipeline and Astropy. Aperture photometry was performed on detected stars using photutils with calibration against 2MASS photometric standards. Ongoing work involves determining individual stellar masses and cluster age from the pre-main-sequence turn-on mass, quantifying mass segregation via variations in the derived Mass Function, etc.

Gemini F2 Dragons photutils Astropy 2MASS IR Photometry Stellar Clusters

Semester Project

Structural & Morphological Analysis of Galaxies

IISER Kolkata

Astronomy Lab Course Project

Comprehensive morphological study of three galaxies representing different Hubble types: NGC 2967 (Sc spiral), NGC 3351 (SBb barred spiral), and NGC 3379 (E1 elliptical). SDSS SkyServer data were processed to obtain surface brightness profiles, model fitting, error and noise analysis, CAS morphological parameters determination, isophote analysis, global Fourier analysis (2D FFT), radial Fourier analysis (Annular 1D FFT), correlation and statistical analysis, radial structural decomposition, and final comparative classification.

Summer Research Intern

Glitch Mitigation in Precessing GW Searches

Institute of Gravitation and Cosmology, University of Portsmouth, UK

Supervisors: Prof. Laura Nuttall & Dr. Rahul Dhurkunde

Summer project on mitigating the effect of instrumental glitches in searches for gravitational waves from precessing compact binary systems. Gained hands-on experience with matched filtering, signal consistency tests, coincident detection, and ranking statistics using PyCBC. Studied the mathematical formalism of harmonic decomposition of precessing waveforms and the construction of precessing template banks. Conducted precessing searches on several days of LIGO O3 data on the CIT cluster, identified GPS times of glitches impacting precessing searches, characterised glitch types, and estimated detection sensitivity for a simulated population of injections at a fixed FAR. Compared the sensitivity improvement of the new model against existing general searches.

PyCBC Matched Filtering Gravitational Waves Precessing Waveforms LIGO O3 Python

Star Formation in Young Cluster NGC 2316

Aryabhatta Research Institute of Observational Sciences (ARIES), Nainital, India

Supervisor: Dr. Saurabh

Observational study of star formation in the young stellar cluster NGC 2316, using the 3.6 m Devasthal Optical Telescope with imaging and spectroscopy with TANSPEC. Performed UVBRI PSF photometry of cluster stars using DAOPHOT II, calibrated with Landolt standard SA 98 to determine true stellar magnitudes.
